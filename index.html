About Osaka

Formerly known as Naniwa and once the capitol city of Japan, Osaka is the second largest metropolitan city after Tokyo. Also hailed as the economic dynamo of the Kansai Region for centuries, its history and culture paired with the mesmerizing sights, world-renowed food, booming nightlife, and overall atmosphere make Osaka a perennial favorite among visitors and Japan connoisseurs, despite being often overlooked.

5 Historical Facts

  1. Osaka castle, built in 1583, was the largest castle in Japan at the time. It played a major role in the unification of Japan.
  2. Naniwa-no-Miya palace, one of the oldest landmarks in Japan, was built in the year 650.
  3. Osaka is known as the "Nation's Kitchen" on account of the delicious food and plethora of street vendors in Dotonburi.
  4. Osaka was officially founded in 1889.
  5. Osaka has roughly 3,395 temples, some dating back to 500AD.
